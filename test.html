What is ulcer?


An Ulcer is an opening or a sore on the internal or external surface of the body, usually caused by a break in the skin or mucous membrane which fails to heal. Ulcers range from small, painful sores in the mouth, to bedsores and serious lesions of the stomach or intestine.

Stomach Ulcers


A stomach ulcer is a sore that develops on the lining of the oesophagus, stomach or small intestine.



How ulcers occur.


Stomach Ulcers occur when acid in the stomach damages the lining of the digestive tract. Other common causes include the bacteria H.Pylori and anti-inflammatory pain relievers including aspirin.
